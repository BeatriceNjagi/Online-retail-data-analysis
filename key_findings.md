# Key Insights — Online Retail Dataset (2011)
## 1. Monthly Revenue Performance (2011)

- November recorded the highest revenue, showing a 12% increase from October.

- This spike is likely due to early Christmas shopping and Black November promotional sales.

- October was the second-highest revenue month.

- The largest month-to-month revenue increase occurred in September (+48%), followed by May (+45%).

- The sharpest revenue decline was observed in December (-55%), likely due to post-holiday slowdowns.

- Additional declines were recorded in February and April (both -21%).

## 2. Country Performance

- Top revenue-generating countries: Netherlands, Eire, and Germany.

- Lowest-performing countries by revenue: Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, and the Czech Republic.

- Regional trend: European countries contributed the highest revenue, while Asian countries showed significantly lower revenue levels.

## 3. Top Customers by Revenue

- The highest-spending customer generated £280,206.02 in total revenue.

- At least one customer recorded £0 total spend, indicating incomplete or non-purchasing transactions.

## 4. Global Product Demand

- Demand trends mirror revenue performance: Netherlands, Eire, and Germany show the highest product demand, aligning with their top revenue positions.

- Lowest product demand was observed in: Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, and RSA, consistent with their revenue ranking.
